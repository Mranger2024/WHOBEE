## The Reality of Anonymous Chat Safety

Anonymous video chat is inherently a space of unpredictability. That's part of its appeal — you never know who you'll meet. But that same unpredictability means you need to approach it with awareness.

The good news: with the right platform and habits, anonymous chatting is safe, fun, and genuinely rewarding.

## Technical Safety: What Your Platform Should Provide

Before downloading any app or opening any website, check for these technical safeguards:

### 1. Peer-to-Peer (P2P) Connections
The gold standard for video privacy. With WebRTC P2P technology (what WHOBEE uses), your video stream goes **directly** to your chat partner. It does not route through company servers, meaning the platform cannot record or view your video.

**Ask the question:** Does this platform route video through its own servers?

### 2. End-to-End Encryption for Text
Text messages should be encrypted before leaving your device. WHOBEE uses ECDH + AES-GCM — an industry-standard key exchange that means even WHOBEE cannot read your messages.

### 3. No Account = Less Risk
Platforms requiring full registration collect your personal data. Anonymous platforms with no sign-up have nothing to breach.

### 4. Clear Reporting Mechanisms
Any legitimate platform should have an obvious, easy-to-use report button. WHOBEE's report button is visible during every chat session.

## Behavioral Safety Tips

Technology alone isn't enough. Your behavior matters.

### Never Share Personal Information
This seems obvious, but it's worth repeating:
- Real full name
- Home or work address
- Phone number
- Social media handles or usernames
- Financial information

Even your school or workplace can be used to narrow down your identity.

### Use the Skip Button Without Hesitation
The best tool for safety is the skip button. If a conversation makes you uncomfortable for **any reason**, skip immediately. You don't owe anyone your time or explanation.

### Don't Let Other Users Pressure You
Legitimate users understand "no." If someone is pressuring you to share information, turn on a camera, or do anything you're not comfortable with — that's a red flag. Skip and report.

### Protect Your Background
Before starting a video chat, look at what's visible behind you. Address details, identifiable landmarks, or family photos can inadvertently reveal your location.

## Red Flags to Watch For

Watch for these warning signs during any anonymous chat session:

- Asking for your social media, email, or phone number early
- Claiming to be from a specific company or authority figure
- Asking you to move the conversation to a different platform immediately
- Any sexual content involving or targeting minors
- Requests for money, even small amounts

If you see any of these, report the user before skipping.
